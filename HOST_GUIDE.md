# 🎭 MURDER MYSTERY HOST GUIDE
## Sand, Secrets & Sorrow: A Cape May Mystery

---

## BEFORE THE GAME STARTS (30 minutes before)

1. **Set up the TV screen** with the game running at the landing page showing all 11 characters
2. **Hand out character assignments** - each player gets their character name on a note or card
3. **Ask players to NOT read the digital game yet** - tell them to wait for Phase 0
4. **Have physical evidence props ready** and hidden in designated locations:
   - Derek's briefcase → sitting room
   - Grammy's medication bottle → kitchen counter  
   - Empty cognac sniffer → visible on a table
   - Financial documents → Derek's room
5. **Brief players once:** "This is a murder mystery. Derek has been found dead. Everyone is a suspect. Check your phone for your character info when Phase 0 starts. Your secrets stay private unless you choose to reveal them."

---

## PHASE 0: WELCOME TO CAPE MAY (5 minutes)
**Time on host screen: ~5:15 PM**

### YOUR ANNOUNCEMENT:
"Welcome to Cape May, everyone. It's Memorial Day weekend, rain is coming down hard, and we've all gathered at Grammy's beach house for a perfect family gathering.

Now check your phones. Find your character. Read your biography, your secret, and your alibi. You have 3 minutes to get into character. Remember: your secret stays secret unless YOU decide to reveal it. This weekend will test all of you."

### WHAT'S HAPPENING:
- Players read their full character profiles on their phones
- They absorb their secrets, aliases, and background
- They get into character mindset
- TV screen shows the game intro message

### DURATION: 3-5 minutes (let them read, don't rush)

### WHAT YOU'RE DOING:
- Watching the room
- Making sure everyone's on their phone reading
- Getting ready to advance to Phase 1

### HOST TIP:
Don't advance until everyone's looked at their phone and seems ready. This is crucial setup time.

---

## PHASE 1: THE DISCOVERY (8:45 PM) (8 minutes)
**Advance to Phase 1 on host screen**

### YOUR ANNOUNCEMENT:
"It's 8:45 PM. Derek is found unresponsive in the sitting room. The paramedics arrive. Derek Hollis is pronounced dead.

This is now a murder investigation. Everyone has secrets. Everyone has motive.

Check your phones for new information. This is your initial evidence. We'll have time to investigate more, but right now: you all know Derek arrived this morning. You all know something was wrong. And now he's dead.

Take 2 minutes to read your new clues on your phone."

### WHAT'S HAPPENING:
- Players see Phase 1 TV message on the big screen
- Players see 5 initial public clues appear on their phones
- The mystery officially begins
- Players start thinking about motives

### DURATION: 8 minutes total
- 2 minutes: quiet reading
- 6 minutes: casual talking/processing

### WHAT YOU'RE DOING:
- Monitor the room energy
- Watch for people making connections
- Listen for people starting to accuse each other
- Don't intervene yet - let them talk naturally

### HOST TIP:
This phase is about setting the tone. People should start asking questions like "Why was Derek here?" and "What was he threatening people about?" Let that conversation happen organically.

---

## PHASE 2: THE INVESTIGATION (12:30 AM) (20 minutes)
**Advance to Phase 2 on host screen**

### YOUR ANNOUNCEMENT:
"It's now Phase 2: The Investigation. New evidence has surfaced.

**First:** Everyone, check your phones right now. You have new clues. Your character's secrets may have expanded. Some of you have additional private information about Derek and what he knew."

*(Wait 2 minutes for everyone to read)*

"**Second:** The beach house is now open for investigation. Derek's room is accessible. The kitchen is accessible. Grammy's room is accessible. If your character wants to search for physical evidence, they can do that now. Physical evidence includes: Derek's briefcase with financial documents, Grammy's medication bottle in the kitchen, financial records, and anything else you find."

*(Point to where evidence is located)*

"**Third:** This is also when side quests become available. Some of you have special investigation goals listed on your phone. These are opportunities to dig deeper into specific mysteries."

### WHAT'S HAPPENING:
- Players get expanded clues and evidence
- Players actively search the house for physical props
- Players start comparing notes more seriously
- Players realize Derek was blackmailing/threatening multiple people
- The scope of the mystery expands

### DURATION: 20 minutes total
- 2 minutes: reading new info
- 15-18 minutes: searching/investigating/discussing
- 2 minutes: wrapping up before Phase 3

### WHAT YOU'RE DOING:
- **Facilitate searching:** Answer questions like "Can I look in Derek's suitcase?" (Yes). "Can I read these documents?" (Yes).
- **Moderate discussions:** Listen as people piece together Derek's threats. Let them debate.
- **Track what they discover:** Pay attention to who finds what evidence.
- **Keep time:** After 15 minutes, start warning: "5 minutes left to investigate. Gather what you need."
- **Don't reveal:** Don't tell them who the killer is. Let them theorize.

### HOST TIP:
This is the most interactive phase. People will be moving around, talking excitedly, realizing Derek was a predator. This energy is GOOD. Let it flow. If someone accuses someone else, say "That's a theory - let's see if the evidence supports it."

**KEY MOMENT:** By the end of Phase 2, people should understand:
- Derek embezzled $340K+ from Grammy
- Derek was blackmailing EVERY FAMILY MEMBER
- Derek had leverage on everyone
- Someone had opportunity AND motive

---

## PHASE 3: CONFRONTATIONS (1:00 AM) (15 minutes)
**Advance to Phase 3 on host screen**

### YOUR ANNOUNCEMENT:
"Phase 3: Confrontations. It's time to make formal accusations.

The gloves are off. You can now directly accuse someone of killing Derek. When you make an accusation, you must present evidence. The accused can respond with their own evidence and alibi challenges.

Here's how it works:
1. Someone makes an accusation: 'I think [character] killed Derek'
2. They present their evidence and reasoning
3. The accused responds with their alibi and counter-evidence
4. Others can jump in with their own clues
5. This is a debate - bring your evidence

You have 15 minutes. Not everyone will be accused. People will break under pressure or hold their ground. Who will crack?"

### WHAT'S HAPPENING:
- Players make direct accusations
- Players debate based on evidence
- Alibis are challenged
- Secrets come out publicly
- The temperature in the room rises
- People start pointing fingers
- The mystery tightens

### DURATION: 15 minutes

### WHAT YOU'RE DOING:
- **Be the moderator:** Make sure people are civil. Let heated debate happen, but keep it respectful.
- **Ask clarifying questions:** "Why do you think [character] had access to the kitchen?" "What evidence supports that?"
- **Play devil's advocate:** "But wait - [character] said they were in the living room. Who can verify that?"
- **Track accusations:** Notice who's being accused most, who's defending themselves, who's staying quiet.
- **Keep time:** "10 minutes left" at the 5-minute mark. "2 minutes, last accusations" at 13 minutes.
- **Don't reveal:** Still don't tell them who did it.

### HOST TIP:
This is dramatic and fun. People will get invested. You might see:
- Someone getting defensive (maybe they have something to hide?)
- Alliances forming ("I believe John because...")
- People changing their minds
- New theories emerging

Let it HAPPEN. This is the climax before the vote.

**RED FLAGS TO WATCH FOR:**
- If no one's accusing anyone → prompt: "So far, no one's been directly accused. Who looks most suspicious?"
- If it's too calm → encourage: "This is your chance to make your case!"
- If it's too chaotic → gently refocus: "Let's hear from [character] about their alibi"

---

## PHASE 4: THE FINAL VOTE (1:15 AM) (10 minutes)
**Advance to Phase 4 on host screen**

### YOUR ANNOUNCEMENT:
"Phase 4: The Final Vote.

You've investigated. You've debated. You've made accusations. Now it's time to vote.

On your phone, you'll see a voting screen. You must choose WHO YOU THINK KILLED DEREK. You must be prepared to explain your reasoning, but right now - cast your vote.

You have 3 minutes to vote. Once everyone has voted, we move to the final phase."

*(Wait for people to click "Cast Ballot" on their phones)*

### WHAT'S HAPPENING:
- Players vote on who they think the killer is
- The host screen shows vote count in real-time: "VOTES CASTING - [X] CAST" and "AWAITING: [names of people who haven't voted]"
- This creates pressure - everyone knows who hasn't voted yet
- Tension builds

### DURATION: 10 minutes total
- 3 minutes: voting
- 3-5 minutes: waiting & discussing while others vote
- 2 minutes: transition to Phase 5

### WHAT YOU'RE DOING:
- **Watch your host screen** to see vote count update
- **Call out progress:** "3 people have voted. 8 still waiting." This creates social pressure.
- **Don't reveal results yet:** You'll see vote tallies but DON'T announce them yet.
- **Encourage stragglers:** "If you haven't voted, head to the voting screen on your phone now."
- **Build tension:** As votes come in, the room will feel the drama building.

### HOST TIP:
The vote count on your screen is public - people can see who voted and who's waiting. This creates natural pressure and psychology. Some people will vote quickly, others will wait to see what others do. Let that play out.

**Once all votes are in:**
"Everyone's voted. The ballots are sealed. In a moment, we're moving to the final phase - The Revelation. We'll see if you got it right... or if the truth is something else entirely."

---

## PHASE 5: THE REVELATION (1:25 AM) (5-10 minutes)
**Advance to Phase 5 on host screen**

### YOUR ANNOUNCEMENT:
"Phase 5: The Revelation.

The murderer was someone you all know. Someone who made a choice to protect their family. Someone who decided that Derek's threats were so dangerous, so pervasive, so evil... that the only way to save everyone was to end it.

Look at the TV screen. Look at the truth."

### WHAT'S HAPPENING:
- The TV screen displays: **"GRAMMY DID IT."**
- Full dramatic narrative reveals how and why she killed Derek
- Players read the killer's motivation
- The twist lands (or confirms their theory)
- Reactions happen

### DURATION: 5-10 minutes
- 5 minutes: TV screen shows full reveal narrative
- 2-5 minutes: discussion & reactions

### WHAT YOU'RE DOING:
- **Let them read** - don't talk while they're reading the reveal
- **Watch reactions** - people will react differently based on whether they guessed right
- **Facilitate post-game discussion:** Once they've read it:
  - "Who guessed correctly?"
  - "What was the key evidence you missed?"
  - "What convinced you it was someone else?"
  - "How do you feel about Grammy's choice?"

### HOST TIP:
This is the payoff. Some people will have guessed correctly. Others will be shocked. Some will realize they were close but missed one key detail.

**OPTIONAL POST-GAME TALK:**
"Grammy discovered Derek was systematically embezzling $340K+ from her. He blackmailed every family member. He threatened to destroy the family through legal action. Grammy made a choice: lose her wealth fighting him in courts, or protect her family. She chose protection. That's the mystery - not just WHO killed him, but WHY."

## QUICK DECISION TREE (if stuck)

**"Who should speak next?"**
- Let whoever wants to speak speak
- If quiet: "John, you've been quiet. What do you think?"

**"Should I tell them to move on?"**
- If Phase feels done → "Let's move to the next phase"
- If people are still engaged → let them keep going (flexible timing is OK)

**"Someone's accusing the wrong person hard"**
- Don't shut them down
- Say: "That's a strong theory. What evidence supports that?"
- Let them defend OR realize they're wrong

**"Someone's getting upset/defensive"**
- This is natural and part of the game
- Gently: "Hey, remember - this is all about the mystery. No one's actually guilty."
- But let them be invested - it's more fun

**"Should I give them hints?"**
- PHASE 0-3: No hints. Let them discover.
- PHASE 4-5: If they're totally stuck before voting, you can say: "Think about who had both motive AND opportunity"
