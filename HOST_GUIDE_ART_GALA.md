# 🎨 THE GILDED DECEPTION: ART GALA MYSTERY
## Ultra-Detailed Host Guide

## BEFORE THE GAME STARTS (45 minutes before)

### Setup & Atmosphere (30 min before)
1. **Set the mood** — Turn on the TV at the landing page showing all 11 guests
2. **Physical evidence ready & hidden:**
   - Vladimir's leather portfolio (antique, burgundy) → sitting room, hidden but findable
   - Arabella's heart medication bottle (digitalis, labeled) → kitchen counter or study
   - Empty cognac snifter → sitting room, visible on side table
   - Art authentication documents → library or study (scattered among books)
   - Forgery analysis reports → Vladimir's room
3. **Create atmosphere:**
   - Soft background music (1920s jazz is perfect)
   - Dim lighting with warm gold tones
   - Mention the rain outside, the exclusivity of the event
4. **Have champagne or sparkling cider available** — people feel fancy at an art gala

### Briefing Players (15 min before)
Gather everyone and say:

*"Welcome to Arabella Ashford-Cross's exclusive Cape May estate. It's an intimate weekend gala celebrating the acquisition of a priceless masterpiece. We're in the 1920s art world — exclusive, sophisticated, dangerous.*

*Tonight, someone arrives with secrets that threaten everyone here. By tomorrow morning, one person will be dead.*

*Check your phone for your character. Read your biography, your secret, your alibi. You have 5 minutes. This is a world of art, money, and carefully kept secrets. Play the part. Embody your character. Your secret stays secret unless YOU reveal it.*

*This weekend will test all of you."*

## PHASE 0: WELCOME TO THE GALA (5 minutes)
**Time on host screen: ~5:15 PM**

### YOUR ANNOUNCEMENT:
*"Welcome to Arabella Ashford-Cross's Cape May estate. It's an intimate weekend gala celebrating the acquisition of a priceless Impressionist masterpiece. The rain hammers against the windows. The champagne flows. The conversation is refined. But beneath the elegance, everyone here carries secrets.*

*Now check your phones. Find your character. Read your biography, your background, your place in this world. You have 3 minutes to get into character. Remember: you're part of high society. You're sophisticated. You're careful about what you reveal. But your secret is yours to share or keep."*

### WHAT'S HAPPENING:
- Players read full character profiles on phones
- They absorb their secrets, aliases, motivation
- They understand what Vladimir knows about them
- They mentally prepare for the tension ahead

### DURATION: 3-5 minutes (don't rush)

### WHAT YOU'RE DOING:
- Watch the room
- Make sure everyone's reading
- Notice who looks nervous (they probably have a big secret)
- Notice who's already getting into character (good sign)
- Get ready to advance

### HOST TIPS:
- **Set the tone:** You're hosting an exclusive, sophisticated event. Your energy should be calm, elegant, controlled.
- **Watch for nervousness:** Some players might already be uncomfortable with their character's secrets. That's perfect — it means they're invested.
- **Don't advance early:** Even if some players finish reading, wait until everyone's ready. This is crucial setup time.

### CHARACTER NOTES TO WATCH FOR:
- **Arabella** (played by someone) will be reading about being the killer. They'll feel weight. That's good — don't reveal anything.
- **Vladimir's player** should look excited but also understand they're the victim. They might try to act mysterious or threatening. That's perfect.
- **Vivienne's player** will know they're a key witness. They might feel special. Use that energy.

## PHASE 1: THE DISCOVERY (8:45 PM) (8 minutes)
**Advance to Phase 1 on host screen**

### YOUR ANNOUNCEMENT:
*"It's 8:45 PM. Vladimir Karpov, a distinguished art dealer, is found unresponsive in the library. The physician is called. Vladimir Karpov is pronounced dead.*

*An empty cognac snifter sits on the side table. There's a faint, bitter smell in the air.*

*This is now a murder investigation. Everyone has secrets. Everyone had access. Everyone has motive.*

*Check your phones for initial clues. You have 2 minutes to read."*

### WHAT'S HAPPENING:
- Phase 1 message appears on TV
- Players get first round of public clues on phones
- The mystery officially begins
- Players start processing: "Wait, what did Vladimir know about me?"
- Tension rises

### DURATION: 8 minutes total
- 2 minutes: quiet reading
- 6 minutes: casual talking/processing/accusation starting

### WHAT YOU'RE DOING:
- **Monitor the room energy** — Is there shock? Confusion? Excitement?
- **Listen for key phrases:**
  - "He knew about my...?" (Great, they're understanding their secrets are exposed)
  - "I didn't do it!" (Too early, but good energy)
  - "Wait, why would Arabella...?" (Someone's already theorizing, good sign)
- **Watch Arabella's player** — They should look calm-but-tense, like they're playing a grieving hostess. If they look panicked, nudge them: "Remember, you're in shock but you're a sophisticated host. Stay composed."
- **Don't intervene yet** — Let them talk naturally
- **Listen for people making connections** — People will start realizing Vladimir had leverage on multiple people

### HOST TIPS:
This phase is about **setting the tone of suspicion**. People should realize:
- Vladimir wasn't just a guest; he was a threat
- He knew things about multiple people
- Someone among them is responsible

### SIDE CONVERSATIONS & REDIRECTS:

**If someone says: "I didn't do it!"**
Response: *"Nobody said you did. But Vladimir clearly had secrets on everyone. The question is: who felt most threatened?"*

**If someone asks: "What exactly did Vladimir do?"**
Response: *"That's what you need to figure out. He was here as an 'art expert,' but he was doing a lot of private conversations. You all felt unsettled by him. Now you need to piece together why."*

**If someone accuses immediately: "Arabella did it!"**
Response: *"Interesting theory. What evidence makes you say that?"* (Don't confirm or deny. Let them build their case.)

**If Arabella's player looks too shaken:**
Response (quietly, aside): *"Remember, you're a sophisticated hostess who just had a guest collapse. You're shocked, yes. But you're composed. You've handled crises before."*

**If Vivienne's player (the key witness) hasn't spoken up yet:**
Response: *"Vivienne, you were around the library earlier. What did you see Vladimir doing?"*

### RED FLAGS TO WATCH:
- **Too much chaos** → Gently refocus: "Let's establish basic facts first."
- **Someone's confused about their secret** → They might ask for clarification. That's fine — tell them to reference their phone under "My Secret" section.
- **Players shutting down** → Some might feel uncomfortable with their character's secrets. Reassure them: "This is theater. Your character's secrets are part of the story. Play it."

## PHASE 2: THE INVESTIGATION (12:30 AM) (20 minutes)
**Advance to Phase 2 on host screen**

### YOUR ANNOUNCEMENT:
*"It's now Phase 2: The Investigation. New evidence has surfaced, and the manor is open for searching.*

*First: Everyone, check your phones right now. You have expanded clues. Your character's secrets may have expanded. Some of you have new information about Vladimir and his methods.*

(Wait 2 minutes for people to read)

*Second: The manor is now open for investigation. The library is accessible. The study is accessible. The bedrooms are accessible. If your character wants to search for physical evidence, you can do that. Look for Vladimir's leather portfolio — it's been missing since his death. Look for Arabella's medication. Look for documents. Anything."*

(Point to where evidence is located)

*Third: Side quests are available. Some of you have special investigation goals on your phone. These are opportunities to dig deeper. Choose one and pursue it.*

### WHAT'S HAPPENING:
- Players get Phase 2 clues about art forgery, authentication, Vladimir's leverage
- Players actively search the house
- They find physical evidence and understand the scope
- They realize: "Oh, Vladimir was threatening multiple people about different things"
- The energy builds from confusion to investigation

### DURATION: 20 minutes total
- 2 minutes: reading new info
- 15-18 minutes: searching/investigating/discussing
- 1-2 minutes: wrapping up

### WHAT YOU'RE DOING:

**Facilitate searching:**
- Player: "Can I open Vladimir's suitcase?" → Yes
- Player: "Can I read these authentication documents?" → Yes
- Player: "Can I take Arabella's medication bottle?" → Yes (that's fine)
- Player: "Can I leave the manor?" → No, it's raining and exclusive

**Monitor discussions:**
Listen as people piece things together. You should hear:
- "So Arabella's paintings are forgeries?"
- "Wait, Vladimir knew about MY finances too?"
- "Who had access to the kitchen... I mean library?"
- "Why would Arabella poison him if she could just... I dunno, kick him out?"

**Track what they discover:**
- Who finds Vladimir's portfolio first? → They become central to the theory
- Who finds the medication? → They might suspect Arabella
- Who finds the authentication documents? → They understand the forgery angle

**Keep time:**
- At 10 minutes: *"You're doing great. Keep investigating. 5 more minutes."*
- At 15 minutes: *"5 minutes left to search. Gather what you need."*
- At 18 minutes: *"Alright, investigations winding down. Collect what you've found. Phase 3 coming up."*

**Guide without revealing:**
- If someone says: "Arabella definitely did it" → "Maybe. What evidence supports that?"
- If someone says: "It must be the con artist" → "Interesting. Why do you think that?"
- If someone seems stuck → "Think about motive. Who had the MOST to lose if Vladimir went public?"

### SIDE CONVERSATIONS & CHARACTER MOMENTS:

**For Constantine (the investigator):**
His secret mentions he's investigating an art fraud network. You might quietly say to him (or have another character ask): *"Constantine, you're experienced with this kind of thing. What do you think about the forgery angle?"* This validates his expertise without giving away the answer.

**For Ludovic (the con artist):**
He'll probably be nervous about being accused. If someone accuses him, say: *"Ludovic, you look very nervous. Something Vladimir said to you?"* This creates dramatic tension without confirming anything.

**For Evangeline (the young heiress):**
She might hide from the investigation. If she does, gently draw her in: *"Evangeline, you were in your room when Vladimir died. But you were with him earlier today. What did he say to you?"*

**For Josephine (the gossip columnist):**
She might try to interview other players. Encourage it: *"Josephine, keep digging. You're a professional at getting people to talk."*

**For Vivienne (the jazz singer):**
She's the key witness. If she hasn't mentioned what she saw, prompt her: *"Vivienne, you were near the library around 8:00 PM. What did you observe about Arabella's behavior?"*

### WHAT THEY SHOULD UNDERSTAND BY END OF PHASE 2:
- Vladimir was not just a casual guest; he was systematically gathering leverage
- At least 8 paintings in Arabella's collection are forgeries
- Vladimir had secrets on nearly everyone: financial irregularities, shady art deals, hidden transactions
- Someone had strong motive AND opportunity to poison him
- The medication came from Arabella's prescription
- The poisoned drink was served in Arabella's private cognac

### HOST TIP:
This is the most **interactive, fun phase**. People will be moving around, excited, realizing the scope of the mystery. Let that energy flow. If someone says, "Wait, I think I figured out who did it," say: *"That's a solid theory. Hold onto it. We'll see if the evidence supports it in confrontations."*

## PHASE 3: CONFRONTATIONS (1:00 AM) (15 minutes)
**Advance to Phase 3 on host screen**

### YOUR ANNOUNCEMENT:
*"Phase 3: Confrontations. It's time to make formal accusations.*

*The gloves are off. You can now directly accuse someone of killing Vladimir. When you make an accusation, you must present evidence and reasoning. The accused can respond with their own evidence and alibis.*

*Here's how it works:*
1. *Someone makes an accusation: 'I think [character] killed Vladimir'*
2. *They present their evidence*
3. *The accused responds with their alibi and counter-evidence*
4. *Others can jump in with their own clues*
5. *This is a debate — bring your evidence*

*You have 15 minutes. Who will crack under pressure? Check your phones for your final clues and personal evidence."*

### WHAT'S HAPPENING:
- Direct accusations begin
- Players debate based on evidence found in Phase 2
- Alibis are challenged
- Secrets come out publicly (or get defended)
- The temperature rises
- People point fingers

### DURATION: 15 minutes

### WHAT YOU'RE DOING:

**Be the moderator:**
- Make sure people are civil (this is heated debate, not personal attacks)
- Let emotions and accusations flow naturally
- Redirect if it gets off-topic

**Ask clarifying questions:**
- "Why do you think Arabella had access to the digitalis?" (Instead of just accepting the accusation)
- "Constantine, you found documents suggesting multiple people knew about forgeries. Does that mean they all had motive?"
- "If Arabella killed Vladimir, how did she manage to serve him the poisoned drink without anyone noticing?"

**Play devil's advocate:**
- Someone accuses Celestine → "But wait, Celestine was with Margot all evening. Who can verify they weren't in the library?"
- Someone says Ludovic did it → "Ludovic, you had strong motive. Where exactly were you at 8:00 PM?"
- Someone points to Josephine → "You discovered Vladimir had leverage on you. But wouldn't you be more likely to bribe him than poison him?"

**Track accusations:**
- Who's being accused most often? Probably Arabella or Ludovic (the obvious suspects)
- Who's defending themselves strongly? → They're invested
- Who's staying quiet? → Maybe they know something, maybe they're scared
- Who's attacking from the sidelines? → They might be trying to deflect from themselves

**Keep time:**
- At 5 minutes: *"Good theories emerging. Keep going."*
- At 8 minutes: *"5 more minutes of accusations."*
- At 13 minutes: *"Last accusations. Make your final case."*

### SIDE CONVERSATIONS & CHARACTER MOMENTS:

**If Arabella is directly accused:**
Have her respond calmly: *"I was hosting a gala. Would I really murder a guest in my own home? I have reputation to protect. I have everything to lose."* (This is technically true but evasive — she DID do it but for exactly these reasons.)

**If Ludovic is accused:**
Have him get defensive: *"Look, yes, Vladimir had leverage on me. But I didn't kill him. I was in the garden avoiding him because I wanted nothing to do with his threats."* (He's telling the truth — he didn't do it, but he wanted to.)

**If Constantine is accused:**
Have him respond analytically: *"I'm trained to notice details. Yes, I saw suspicious things. But I also recognize that Arabella had the most to lose and the access to execute it."* (He's perceptive; let him defend himself with intelligence.)

**If Josephine is accused:**
Have her laugh nervously: *"I'm a journalist, darling. I expose secrets; I don't poison people. Though I'm interested in whoever does."* (She's evasive but clever.)

**If Evangeline breaks down:**
This is beautiful drama. Let her: *"Vladimir threatened my whole family! I couldn't sleep! But I didn't do anything. I couldn't..."* (She had motive but not the courage.)

**If Vivienne reveals what she saw:**
This is the key moment. Have her speak carefully: *"I saw Arabella go to the library with purpose around 8:00 PM. Not casual. Not grieving. Purposeful. And then... Vladimir was found."* (This is her big contribution.)

**If Margot speaks up:**
Have her be knowing and sophisticated: *"In my experience with... complicated transactions, people who eliminate threats do so with absolute composure. I observed that composure in Arabella after Vladimir's body was found."* (She's smart and hints at understanding without accusing.)

### RED FLAGS TO WATCH:

**If no one's accusing anyone:**
Prompt: *"So far, we have theories but no direct accusations. Who looks most suspicious based on the evidence?"*

**If it's too calm:**
Encourage heated debate: *"Come on. You all have secrets Vladimir was using against you. This is personal. Someone here felt cornered. Who was it?"*

**If it's too chaotic:**
Gently refocus: *"Let's hear from [character] about their alibi. [Character], where exactly were you between 8:00 and 8:30 PM?"*

**If someone gets genuinely upset:**
Gently remind them: *"Remember, this is all about the mystery. Your character might be guilty or innocent, but none of this is real. You're all doing great."*

**If someone wants to accuse multiple people:**
Say: *"You can make multiple accusations, but each needs its own evidence. Let's focus on one at a time."*

### WHAT SHOULD BE HAPPENING:
By the end of Phase 3, people should have built competing theories:
- **Team Arabella:** "She had the most to lose, access to medication, and opportunity"
- **Team Ludovic:** "He's a con artist with motive and he was acting weird"
- **Team Celestine:** "Business rivalry, she wanted to damage Arabella"
- **Scattered votes:** Some people might still be unsure

This is PERFECT. Confusion going into the vote is exciting.

### HOST TIP:
This phase is dramatic and fun. People get invested. You might see alliances forming, people changing their minds, new theories emerging. **Let it happen.** This is the climax before the vote. Your job is to keep it moving and ensure everyone has a voice.

## PHASE 4: THE FINAL VOTE (1:15 AM) (10 minutes)
**Advance to Phase 4 on host screen**

### YOUR ANNOUNCEMENT:
*"Phase 4: The Final Vote.*

*You've investigated. You've debated. You've made accusations. Now it's time to vote.*

*On your phone, you'll see a voting screen. You must choose WHO YOU THINK KILLED VLADIMIR. Cast your vote.*

*You have 3 minutes to vote. Once everyone has voted, we move to the final phase."*

(Wait for people to click "Cast Ballot" on their phones)

### WHAT'S HAPPENING:
- Players vote on who they think the killer is
- The host screen shows vote count in real-time
- People know who's voted and who's waiting
- Tension builds as votes come in

### DURATION: 10 minutes total
- 3 minutes: voting
- 3-5 minutes: waiting & discussing
- 2 minutes: transition to Phase 5

### WHAT YOU'RE DOING:

**Watch your host screen:**
You'll see vote tallies updating. Track them but don't reveal results yet.

**Call out progress:**
- "2 people have voted. 9 awaiting."
- "5 votes cast. 6 still thinking."
- This creates natural social pressure. People are influenced by knowing others have voted.

**Don't reveal results:**
You can see vote tallies but DON'T announce who's winning. The suspense is important.

**Encourage stragglers:**
- *"If you haven't voted, head to the voting screen on your phone now."*
- *"Take your time. Think carefully about who you believe is guilty."*

**Build tension:**
- *"Votes are coming in. The picture is becoming clear... or is it?"*
- *"Interesting. I'm seeing a consensus emerging. Or am I?"*

**Manage the wait time:**
If voting is taking longer than 3 minutes, have a side conversation:
- Ask someone: *"What's your reasoning for voting the way you did?"*
- Have them explain their theory while others vote
- This builds anticipation

### ONCE ALL VOTES ARE IN:

*"Everyone's voted. The ballots are sealed. In a moment, we're moving to the final phase — The Revelation. We'll see if you got it right... or if the truth is something else entirely."*

(Pause for effect)

*"Are you ready?"*

### HOST TIP:
The vote is theater. Even if everyone votes for the wrong person, the reveal is satisfying because the truth is shocking. Your job is to build suspense and make them feel like their votes mattered.

## PHASE 5: THE REVELATION (1:25 AM) (5-10 minutes)
**Advance to Phase 5 on host screen**

### YOUR ANNOUNCEMENT:
*"Phase 5: The Revelation.*

*The murderer was someone you all know. Someone who made a choice to protect what mattered most. Someone who decided that Vladimir's threats were so dangerous, so pervasive, so destructive... that the only way to protect everything was to end it.*

*Look at the TV screen. Look at the truth."*

### WHAT'S HAPPENING:
- The TV displays the full killer reveal narrative
- Players read about Arabella's decision to poison Vladimir
- The twist lands
- Reactions happen

### DURATION: 5-10 minutes
- 5 minutes: TV shows full reveal narrative
- 2-5 minutes: discussion & reactions

### WHAT YOU'RE DOING:

**Let them read:**
Don't talk while they're reading the reveal. Let the narrative land. It's dramatic. It's personal. It explains everything.

**Watch reactions:**
- Who looks shocked? (They didn't guess)
- Who looks satisfied? (They were right)
- Who looks disappointed? (They were close)
- Who looks confused? (The twist surprised them)

These reactions are beautiful. Enjoy them.

**Facilitate post-game discussion:**
Once they've all read, open it up:

*"So... thoughts? Reactions? Who got it right?"*

Ask specific questions:
- **"Did anyone guess Arabella correctly?"** (Celebrate them)
- **"What was the key evidence you missed?"** (Discuss the details)
- **"What convinced you it was [wrong suspect]?"** (Analyze their reasoning)
- **"How do you feel about Arabella's choice?"** (This is the emotional question)

### SIDE CONVERSATIONS:

**For Arabella's player:**
After the reveal, pull them aside quietly: *"Great performance. The way you stayed composed while everyone was accusing you was perfect. Did you drop any hints?"*

**For Vivienne's player:**
*"You were the key witness. Your observations were exactly what people needed. How did you feel holding that knowledge?"*

**For Ludovic's player:**
*"You were the perfect red herring. People wanted to believe you did it. How did that feel?"*

**For the person who guessed right:**
*"You figured it out. What was the moment you realized it was Arabella?"*

**For people who were completely wrong:**
*"What was your theory? Why were you convinced? It's interesting how certain people can seem guilty when they're actually innocent."*

### HOST TIP - THE EMOTIONAL ANGLE:
This is the moment to discuss not just WHO did it, but WHY. The reveal isn't just "Arabella poisoned Vladimir." It's:

- Arabella's forty-year reputation was everything to her
- The forgeries threatened to destroy her completely
- Vladimir wasn't going to back down
- She made an impossible choice
- She chose her legacy over her morality

This is SHAKESPEAREAN. Play it that way.

### OPTIONAL POST-GAME DEBRIEF:
*"Arabella discovered that her prized collection — the centerpiece of her reputation — was built on forgeries. Not just the Monet. Eight other paintings. Her entire legacy was a lie.*

*Vladimir had documentation. He was going to expose it to the world. Arabella's reputation, her finances, her place in high society — all of it gone.*

*She made a choice: lose everything fighting Vladimir in court, or protect her legacy. She chose protection. That's the mystery — not just WHO killed him, but WHY. And whether her choice was justified."*

## QUICK DECISION TREE

**"Who should speak next?"**
- Let whoever wants to speak speak
- If quiet: "Josephine, you're a journalist. What's your theory?"
- If someone's been silent: "Constantine, what do you make of the timeline?"

**"Should I move to the next phase?"**
- Phase feels done → "Let's move forward"
- People still engaged → Let them keep going (flexible timing is OK)

**"Someone's accusing the wrong person hard"**
- Don't shut them down
- Ask: "That's an interesting theory. What evidence supports that specifically?"
- Let them defend OR realize they're wrong

**"Someone looks uncomfortable with their character"**
- Quietly: "You're doing great. Play it. This is theater."
- Reassure them: "Your character's secrets are part of the story."

**"Should I give hints?"**
- PHASE 0-2: No hints. Let them discover.
- PHASE 3: Subtle nudges are OK. Ask clarifying questions.
- PHASE 4: If they're totally stuck before voting: "Think about who had both motive AND opportunity."

## CHARACTER-SPECIFIC HOSTING NOTES

### Arabella Ashford-Cross (The Killer — Secret from Host)
**In Phase 0-3:** She should play a sophisticated, slightly panicked hostess. She's grieving Vladimir's death but also relieved. Make sure her player understands the contrast.

**Red flags:**
- If she's TOO panicked → Remind her she's a composed woman
- If she's TOO calm → People might think she's fake grieving

**During confrontations:** If directly accused, let her defend herself calmly. She's innocent-acting because she's acting.

**Post-reveal:** Celebrate her performance. She was subtle and convincing.

### Vladimir Karpov (The Victim)
**In Phase 1 (before he's dead):** He's already gone, so there's no live performance. But his presence haunts the game. His secrets are the centerpiece.

**What players should understand:** He was systematic, calculated, and threatening. He held leverage like currency.

### Vivienne Dumont (Key Witness)
**Throughout the game:** She should seem observant and slightly amused by the drama. She's the one who sees Arabella's moment.

**In Phase 3:** If she hasn't revealed what she saw, directly prompt her: "Vivienne, what did you observe about Arabella around 8:00 PM?"

**Post-reveal:** She should feel validated. Her observations were crucial.

### Ludovic Steele (Red Herring)
**Throughout the game:** He should seem nervous and evasive. He has motive (Vladimir was a threat), so people will suspect him.

**In Phase 3:** When accused, let him get defensive. It makes him seem guiltier. Then, when the reveal happens, the audience realizes he was innocent all along.

**Post-reveal:** People will feel clever for suspecting him, even though he's innocent. That's the point of a red herring.

### Constantine Blackwell (The Investigator)
**Throughout the game:** He should be analytical and observant. He notices things others miss.

**In Phase 2:** He might find key evidence. Let him. His discoveries help build the case.

**In Phase 3:** When he analyzes the evidence, take him seriously. He's intelligent and his observations are valuable.

### Evangeline Beaumont (Scared Heiress)
**Throughout the game:** She should seem frightened and out of her depth. Vladimir threatened her inheritance.

**In Phase 1-2:** She might hide or seem withdrawn. That's fine; it fits her character.

**In Phase 3:** If she breaks down emotionally, let it happen. She had motive but not the nerve.

### Josephine Marchmont (Gossip Columnist)
**Throughout the game:** She should be extracting information, asking questions, trying to understand the bigger story.

**In Phase 2-3:** She might confront people about what Vladimir knew. Let her. That's her character.

### Margot Silvain (Sophisticated Observer)
**Throughout the game:** She should be cool, knowing, never quite revealing how much she understands.

**In Phase 3:** Her observations should be vague but insightful. She "understands leverage."

### Maximilian Creed (Witty Playwright)
**Throughout the game:** He's analyzing human behavior. He notices inconsistencies.

**In Phase 3:** His accusations should be intelligent and detailed. He understands motivation.

### Quinton Darling (Wealthy Aesthete)
**Throughout the game:** He's amused by the drama but also perceptive. He notices things about people.

**In Phase 3:** He might make sharp observations about character and motive.

### Celestine Vanderbilt (Rival Collector)
**Throughout the game:** She should seem competitive and slightly angry. Vladimir threatened her professionally.

**In Phase 3:** When accused, she should defend herself intelligently. She had motive but not opportunity.
